Allegory of the City of Madrid is an 1810 oil painting by the Spanish artist Francisco Goya presenting an allegory of Madrid, the capital of the Kingdom of Spain. The painting was produced during the Peninsular War, when Goya received patronage from the French-installed monarch Joseph Bonaparte.
